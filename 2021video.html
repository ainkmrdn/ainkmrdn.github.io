CAP RAMBUTAN DANCE
Viral Meter:

Who would have thought that by uploading a video of himself dancing on TikTok a young man managed to attract the attention of a well-known manufacturing company? Recently, social media was abuzz after a video of TikTok user, Shazani Shamsor dancing to the iconic Beras Cap Rambutan ad song made its way online.To date, the video has garnered 1.7 million views and over 7,500 comments from netizens on TikTok. In this case, a lot of them couldn’t help but be entertained with his expressionless dancing. Interestingly enough, Syazani also received a direct message from the rice company on Instagram. They offered to give him and his family a sack of rice for free as a gift!In an interview with VOCKET, the 19-year-old spoke about receiving the offer from the folks at Beras Cap Rambutan following the unexpected success of the video. The company said that they wanted to reward his creativity in coming up with the TikTok post.

Viral Meter:

Ching chong eyes!" That's what elementary school kids used to call Sophie Wang. It was an insidious racist slur casually thrown around as they mocked her Asian ethnicity while pulling on the corner of their eyes. Upward for Japanese. To the side for Chinese. Downward for Korean. Wang is now 17 and many years removed from the days when her Asian American identity was reduced to "a single facial feature." And yet, scrolling through social media posts in recent months has brought those memories flooding back thanks to a new beauty trend: "fox eyes." On Instagram, TikTok and YouTube, people from all over the world have been posting videos modeling the look -- using makeup and other tactics to emulate the lifted, so-called "almond-shaped" eyes of celebrities such as Kendall Jenner, Bella Hadid and Megan Fox
